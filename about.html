About me

My name is Robert and I am 44 years old. I first started to ride motocross when I was only six years old. However I stoped when I became a teenager. Several years later, at the age of 33, I started to ride again. This time with a lot of passion for the sport. I started to ride both motocross and enduro. I practied hard after a couple of years I dared to enter a competition. It was actually an endur race. Later that year I also participated in motocross competitions. Is just on an amatuer level, but in about three yeas I managed to progress from class D to class B. Almost made it to class A a couple of races. Sadly for about two years ago I started to get really sick because of a chronical liver disease. In the spring of 2023 I went through a liver transplantation and started rehab. In the start of August I was back on the dirtbike again, and will start to train for the amatuer races again. My goal is to reach class A.

Motocross vs. Enduro

The main different between the sports is that motocross is on a track with a everyone starting behind a start gate. Enduro is to ride on marked trails. Usually in a forest, but that can vary a lot. Enduro is mostly longer races and can take a whole day to complete. Some races are even 24h races.
Another thing that is different is the dirtbike itself. A motocross bike are more rapid in throttle response, are more aggressive to ride and have a stiffer suspension. An enduro can be recognized that is often has a headlight and a break light in the rear. Also common with a horn.
